**Mitigation:**
- FF Steel produces Grade 80 rebar (premium product) — can likely command higher prices
- Owns grid stations (reduces reliability risk, may not reduce tariff impact)
- KPK may have slightly lower industrial tariffs than Sindh/Punjab (unverified)

**Source trace:** PALSP data via The News, Brecorder; FF Steel website (132kV grid station mention)

## MANAGEMENT MEETING INTERROGATION BRIEF

### Top 10 Questions — Ranked by Intelligence Value

**Q1: "The IPO was announced in September 2021. It's now February 2026. What specifically prevented listing in 2022, 2023, 2024, and 2025 — and what has changed to make it viable NOW?"**
- **Why it matters:** A 4+ year delay is the single biggest red flag. Tests whether management can provide a credible chronology
- **Red flag response:** "Market conditions weren't favorable" — then ask: "KSE-100 hit all-time highs in 2024-2025. Why not then?"
- **Good answer:** Specific timeline with specific blockers — "FY22: IMF crisis froze capital markets. FY23: SBP import restrictions. FY24: conversion to public limited required audit restructuring. FY25: SECP approval process. FY26: book-building scheduled for [date]"

---

**Q2: "Can you share audited financial statements for FY23, FY24, and FY25? The most recent publicly available data is FY22."**
- **Why it matters:** The 3-4 year data gap is extraordinary for a company entering public markets. Tests whether recent performance supports the IPO valuation
- **Red flag response:** "We'll share during the IPO process" — which means investors are being asked to commit to "soon" without seeing 3 years of performance
- **Good answer:** Immediate provision of audited financials with commentary on margin trajectory

---

**Q3: "Your gross margin was 14.8% in FY21 and 13.4% in FY22. With the 267% increase in energy costs since FY21, what are your margins NOW?"**
- **Why it matters:** This is the single most important financial question. If margins held at 13-15% despite energy costs tripling, FF Steel has genuine pricing power. If they've compressed to 6-9% (peer levels), the investment thesis changes
- **Red flag response:** "Margins have been under pressure but we're managing" — needs numbers
- **Good answer:** "FY25 gross margin was X%, driven by Grade 80 premium pricing of Y% over Grade 60, plus scrap cost decline of Z%"

---

**Q4: "What is the current status of the Karachi 3rd plant and the Special Economic Zone? These were the stated IPO objectives in 2021."**
- **Why it matters:** If PKR 9B was supposed to fund these projects and neither has started, what is the IPO money actually for NOW?
- **Red flag response:** "Plans have evolved" — meaning the 2021 use of proceeds has changed. What's the new use?
- **Good answer:** "Karachi site acquired, approvals at stage X, expected commissioning [date]. SEZ at stage Y. Here's the updated capital allocation plan"

---

**Q5: "Your Chairman serves on the SNGPL board, KPK government committees, and was a Senator until 2021. How will you manage conflicts of interest as a public company? What related-party transaction policies are in place?"**
- **Why it matters:** Institutional investors will scrutinize governance. A founder-Senator-Chairman who sits on government energy boards while running a company whose #1 cost is energy creates obvious conflict-of-interest perception
- **Red flag response:** "There are no conflicts" — this is naive and suggests lack of readiness for public market scrutiny
- **Good answer:** "We have established an independent audit committee chaired by [non-family member], related-party transactions are disclosed per [standard], and the Chairman has recused himself from SNGPL energy pricing decisions affecting our sector"

---

**Q6: "Amreli lost PKR 6.1 billion in FY24. Mughal's margins collapsed to 0.5%. How did FF Steel maintain profitability during the same period, and is it sustainable?"**
- **Why it matters:** Being an outlier is either a strength (genuine competitive advantage) or a data question (private company financials not yet subject to public audit). Tests whether management can articulate WHY they're different
- **Red flag response:** "We focus on quality" — generic, doesn't explain the 10pp margin gap
- **Good answer:** "Three specific factors: (1) Grade 80 commands PKR X premium per ton, (2) Peshawar plant has [specific] energy advantage, (3) our capacity utilization was Y% vs industry average of Z%"

---

**Q7: "What is your capacity utilization rate? You have 400,000 tons annual capacity — what is actual production, and what drives the gap?"**
- **Why it matters:** Capacity vs production reveals demand reality. If utilization is 50-60%, there's significant demand risk. If 80%+, the company needs the Karachi plant
- **Red flag response:** "We operate near capacity" — without numbers
- **Good answer:** "FY25 production was X tons, utilization at Y%. Peshawar runs at A%, Lahore at B%"

---

**Q8: "What percentage of your revenue comes from Grade 80 vs Grade 60? And what's the margin differential?"**
- **Why it matters:** If Grade 80 is the moat but represents only 5% of revenue, it's a marketing tool not a business driver
- **Red flag response:** "It's a growing segment" — needs quantification
- **Good answer:** "Grade 80 is X% of revenue at Y% gross margin. Grade 60 is Z% at W% margin. We expect Grade 80 to reach A% by FY27 due to [specific projects]"

---

**Q9: "Your copper ingot business in Gujranwala — what's its contribution to revenue and profit? Is this a core business or a side venture?"**
- **Why it matters:** A steel company with a copper operation raises questions about focus. Is it meaningful or a distraction?
- **Red flag response:** "It's a small operation" — then why mention it in your company profile?
- **Good answer:** "Copper contributes PKR X billion in export revenue at Y% margin. It's strategically important because [specific reason]"

---

**Q10: "Post-IPO, with family retaining 75% ownership, what board independence measures will be implemented? Who are the proposed independent directors?"**
- **Why it matters:** For public market credibility, the board needs non-family voices. Tests institutional readiness
- **Red flag response:** "We'll appoint independent directors as per SECP requirements" — minimal compliance vs genuine governance
- **Good answer:** Names and backgrounds of proposed independent directors, audit committee charter, and specific governance policies
