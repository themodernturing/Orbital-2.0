**Rebar-Scrap Spread (the "processing margin"):**

| Component | Current Price (Feb 2026) | Trend |
|---|---|---|

| Scrap (heavy) | PKR 180-190/kg (~PKR 185K/ton) | Down from PKR 260+ in early 2025 |
| **Processing spread** | **PKR 40-50/kg (~PKR 45K/ton)** | **Expanding** ↑ |

**Spread Analysis:**
| Period | Rebar Price Est. | Scrap Price Est. | Spread | Direction |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Mid-2024 | PKR 266-268/ton (EXW) | ~PKR 220K/ton | ~PKR 46-48K | Baseline |
| Late 2025 | ~PKR 250K/ton | ~PKR 185K/ton | ~PKR 65K | **↑ Expanding** |
| Feb 2026 | ~PKR 230K/ton | ~PKR 185K/ton | ~PKR 45K | Stable |

**What This Means:**
- The rebar-scrap spread has stabilized at PKR 45-65K/ton — this is the raw "margin physics" of the industry
- But this is the INDUSTRY spread. FF Steel's actual margins depend on:
  - **Grade 80 premium** over standard rebar pricing (adds to the spread)
  - **Energy cost per ton** (subtracts from the spread — PKR 42K/ton for industry)
  - **Yield efficiency** (how much finished rebar per ton of scrap input)
  - **Transport costs** (Peshawar location: advantage for KPK, disadvantage for Karachi/Lahore markets)

**Lag Analysis:** When scrap prices fall, do rebar prices follow immediately? Current data suggests rebar is sticky downward (PKR 230K stable while scrap dropped PKR 75K). This implies **temporary margin expansion** that may tighten as competition passes through lower input costs.

**Source trace:** Icons.com.pk, KamranSteel, ApproachPakistan, ProPakistani

### SIGNAL 11: WORKING CAPITAL PHYSICS [Grade U — UNOBSERVED] — NEW IN V2
**Confidence: UNOBSERVED — Framework defined, data required from company**

**Why This Matters for Steel:**
Steel companies die from working capital stress, not revenue decline. The cycle: buy imported scrap (USD-denominated, advance payment or LC) → hold inventory → process → sell on 30-90 day credit → collect. Any break in this cycle causes liquidity crises. Amreli's PKR 6.1B loss was partly WC-driven (suspended operations due to "unavailability of working capital lines").

**Working Capital Framework to Request:**

| Metric | Ask | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| **Inventory days** | Average inventory holding (days) for FY23-FY25 | Steel inventory is expensive and price-volatile; long holding = high risk |
| **Receivable days** | Average collection period | Pakistan construction: 60-120 day terms common; longer = WC squeeze |
| **Payable days** | Average supplier payment period | Scrap is imported (30-day LC terms typical) |
| **Cash conversion cycle** | Inventory + Receivables - Payables | Target: ≤ 45 days for healthy operations |
| **Short-term borrowing** | Working capital lines, running finance, LC facilities | Quantum relative to turnover indicates dependence |
| **Emergency borrowing** | Any unplanned short-term facilities in FY23-FY25 | Red flag for liquidity stress |

**What Would Concern Us:** CCC > 60 days, short-term borrowing > 25% of revenue, any emergency facility usage.

**What Would Reassure Us:** CCC ≤ 45 days, declining reliance on short-term lines, positive operating cash flow.
