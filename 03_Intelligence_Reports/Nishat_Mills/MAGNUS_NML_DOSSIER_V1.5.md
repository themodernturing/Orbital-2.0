**Confidence: HIGH — Peer-triangulated across 3 companies, multiple periods**

**Peer Gross Margin Comparison:**

| Company | FY24 Gross Margin | FY25 Gross Margin | Trend | Exports FY24 |
|---|---|---|---|---|

**The Signal:**
All three companies face the same macro environment (cotton costs, energy prices, PKR dynamics). Yet:
- **Interloop** maintains margins 2.5x Nishat's (even after its own decline)
- **Gul Ahmed** maintains margins 1.5x Nishat's AND improved YoY
- **Nishat** has the lowest margins AND the lowest exports

This confirms the margin problem is **company-specific**, not purely sector-driven. Nishat has a structural cost or pricing disadvantage relative to peers.

**Source trace:**
- Nishat FY24 GM: PSX NML Annual Financial Statements FY24, Gross Profit PKR 17.32B / Sales PKR 160.26B
- Interloop FY24 GM: PSX ILPL Financial Statements FY24 (27.89% per PSX financial highlights page)
- Gul Ahmed FY24 GM: Mettis Global analysis of GATM consolidated FY24 results (16.66%)
- Interloop FY25 GM: PSX ILPL Financial Statements FY25 (20.29%); Arif Habib analysis confirms 65.7% PAT decline
- Gul Ahmed FY25 GM: Mettis Global analysis of GATM consolidated FY25 results (17.29%)

> **Gross Margin Definition:** Gross Profit / Net Revenue, per unconsolidated financial statements for NML, consolidated for GATM. Quarterly margin volatility (Q2 FY24 NML showing 33.52%) likely reflects seasonal product mix differences (higher-margin processed cloth in winter vs lower-margin grey cloth in summer), segment-level vs company-level reporting, or exceptional items. The full-year figure (10.81%) is the reliable baseline.

**What Would Change My Mind:** If Nishat's low margin is a deliberate strategy (high-volume, low-margin) with superior asset turnover, total return on capital could still be competitive. Need to check: ROIC vs peers, not just margin.

## MANAGEMENT MEETING INTERROGATION BRIEF

### Top 10 Questions — Ranked by Intelligence Value

**Q1: "Your gross margin is 10-11% while Interloop is at 20-28% and Gul Ahmed is at 16-17%. All three of you face the same input costs. What specifically explains this gap?"**
- **Why it matters:** Directly challenges the "industry-wide" excuse with peer data
- **Red flag response:** "We compete in different segments" — then follow up: "Show us the margin per segment"
- **Good answer:** Specific decomposition by product line and cost center

**Q2: "Revenue grew 16% but PAT fell 26% in H1 FY25, attributed to 'lower other income.' What was this other income, and should we treat it as recurring?"**
- **Why it matters:** Tests structural dependency on non-core income
- **Red flag response:** "Other income varies period to period"
- **Good answer:** Specific decomposition — "PKR X from associates, PKR Y was one-off"

**Q3: "You've issued a PKR 7 billion Sukuk for working capital while simultaneously investing PKR 5 billion in dairy and building subsidiaries in 3 countries. How is this being funded?"**
- **Why it matters:** Tests capital allocation discipline and leverage risk
- **Red flag response:** "These are separate decisions" — press: "Show us the funding for each"
- **Good answer:** "Sukuk covers seasonal WC. Expansion funded from retained earnings at X rate"

**Q4: "Solar is 38MW but contributes only 7% of your energy. At PKR 35-38/kWh for grid power, what is your timeline to reach 30% solar?"**
- **Why it matters:** Tests if solar is real strategy or PR
- **Red flag response:** "We're continuously evaluating"
- **Good answer:** "We need X MW more, at PKR Y cost, in phases by FY27/FY28"

**Q5: "Your cost of sales surged 54% in Q2 FY24. Can you break this down: cotton, energy, labor, processing — with % for each?"**
- **Why it matters:** Without decomposition, can't assess which costs are controllable
- **Red flag response:** "Our cost accounting captures blended costs"
- **Good answer:** Granular breakdown by input category

**Q6: "What is the expected margin impact of the Roshan Maeshat energy package? Can you quantify the PKR savings per kWh and total annual impact?"**
- **Why it matters:** This is the single biggest potential catalyst
- **Red flag response:** "We're still evaluating eligibility" — months after launch
- **Good answer:** "PKR X billion annual savings starting [date], Y basis points margin improvement"

**Q7: "Corduroy, workwear, and technical fabrics — what is the expected margin profile of each vs your current 11% blended margin?"**
- **Why it matters:** Tests if expansion is margin-accretive or just revenue-additive
- **Red flag response:** "We expect healthy margins" without numbers
- **Good answer:** "Corduroy X%, workwear Y%, vs current 11%. Mix shift target: Z% by FY27"

**Q8: "PKR 5 billion in dairy. What are the returns to date, and what is the strategic rationale for a textile company investing in dairy during margin compression?"**
- **Why it matters:** Classic conglomerate value destruction pattern
- **Red flag response:** "Long-term strategic investment"
- **Good answer:** Specific revenue, profit, break-even date, strategic tie-in

**Q9: "Your interim report cites Bangladesh instability redirecting orders to Pakistan. How much of your H1 FY25 revenue increase was from redirected Bangladesh orders vs organic growth?"**
- **Why it matters:** If growth is windfall (Bangladesh) rather than structural, it's not sustainable
- **Red flag response:** "It's difficult to isolate"
- **Good answer:** "We estimate X% of incremental orders came from Bangladesh reallocation"

**Q10: "What is your customer concentration? What percentage of export revenue comes from your top 3 buyers (H&M, Gap, etc.)?"**
- **Why it matters:** High buyer concentration = pricing power disadvantage
- **Red flag response:** Refusal to disclose or "we serve a diversified portfolio"
- **Good answer:** Specific % per top customer with trend direction
