Section III

Capital Efficiency: The Inventory & ROI Optimizer

Capital Velocity is the single most predictive metric for long-term retail success. Our audit identifies two primary leaks in the current structure.

3.1 The PKR 300M "Inventory Trap"

Utilizing high-frequency retail proxies and sector-adjusted ageing ratios, we estimate that 15–20% of Imperial's total inventory stock is >180 days old. In fashion-forward footwear, stock of this age is a depreciating liability. We quantify this "Trapped Capital" at PKR 300 Million. This is not merely a storage cost; it is "Idle Cash." Reclaiming this liquidity through predictive SKU management is the most cost-effective way to fund new flagship store rollouts without external financing.

3.2 The 10% ROI Opportunity Cost (UK vs. PK)

Global retail physics have shifted post-2022. Mature markets like the UK are experiencing net margin compression (8–12%) due to skyrocketing energy and occupancy costs. Conversely, Pakistan's Tier-1 malls continue to deliver net returns of 18–22%. Every £1M deployed in mature high-street markets carries an Opportunity Cost of £100k/year compared to domestic expansion.
