What do these scores mean?

MSRI (Market Structural Risk Index) measures how risky this company's overall position is, from 0 to 100. A score of 0 means very strong with no major concerns. A score of 100 means severe risk. FF Steel's score of 54 means: a strong company, but with serious unanswered questions that need to be resolved.

NDI (Narrative Divergence Index) measures how well the company's public claims match the actual evidence we found, from 0 to 100. A score of 0 means everything the company says checks out perfectly. A score of 100 means nothing checks out. FF Steel's score of 65 means: some important claims do not match what the evidence shows.

MSRI Composition Appendix

How the Market Structural Risk Index score is calculated

How to Read the Score

Each risk area gets a score from 0 to 100:
  0   = Very strong / clear competitive advantage
  25  = Good, with some small uncertainties
  50  = Cannot tell (not enough data to judge)
  75  = Concerning / real risk exists here
  100 = Severe risk / major problem

The final MSRI is a weighted average of all 12 risk areas.
Grade A signals (high confidence) count fully.
Grade B signals (moderate confidence) count at 70%.
Grade U signals (no data yet) count at 10%.

Floor Rule: If the company's financial data is not available (Signal 1 scores
60 or higher), the MSRI cannot go below 50 — no matter how strong the other
signals are. This is because investors cannot properly evaluate a company
without seeing the financial statements.

Step 3: Signal Scoring & MSRI Calculation

Each risk area is scored based on weight of evidence, not opinion:

  • A score of 15 means: multiple sources confirm, strong evidence → low risk
  • A score of 80 means: no data found, company claims unverified → high risk
  • Each signal gets a risk score × importance weight × data-confidence multiplier
  • The weighted average produces the final MSRI (0-100 scale)
  • Floor rule: If audited financials are missing, MSRI cannot drop below 50 regardless of other positive signals

Step 4: Prediction Registry & Self-Correction

Every version of this report makes testable predictions. When actual data arrives (e.g., IPO prospectus, audited financials), we test whether our estimates were right. This is what transforms speculation into intelligence — the system learns from its own predictions.
