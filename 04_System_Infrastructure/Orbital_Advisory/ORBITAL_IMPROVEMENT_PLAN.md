## WHAT WORKS (KEEP)

| Element | Why It Works | Source |
|---|---|---|
| **"What You Likely Believe" vs. "What Data Shows" framing** | Creates immediate executive engagement. CEOs react to the gap, not the data | Imperial Footwear |
| **Phase 0 = zero internal data** | The amazement factor IS the conversion mechanism. "You found this without any of our data?" | Magnus V3 principle |
| **ORI + BDI scoring** | Numbers create urgency narratives don't. "Your ORI is 42" triggers action faster than 3 pages | ORBITAL_MASTER_SYSTEM_V2 |
| **Strengths-first executive summary** | Balanced framing is more persuasive. CEOs don't trust reports that only attack | FF Steel V3 lesson |
| **10 standardized signals** | Consistency across sectors builds system credibility. Same framework, different calibration | ORBITAL V2 design |
| **Blind Spot Register** | "We found what you don't know" is a more powerful sell than "we found problems" | Imperial + PASHA |
| **Competitive Position Matrix** | Side-by-side peer comparison is immediately actionable | Imperial + PASHA |

## ALL LESSONS LEARNED (Updated per engagement)

---

### From Imperial Footwear (Report #1)

| # | Lesson | Impact |
|---|---|---|
| OL-01 | **Reports beat dashboards for first engagement** — companies saw through simulations | Fundamental product design |
| OL-02 | **Lead with findings, not features** — "your brand is rated 1.7/5" beats "we have a sentiment analysis engine" | Pitch strategy |
| OL-03 | **The "What You Likely Believe" vs "What Data Shows" framing is devastating** — immediate executive engagement | Core methodology |
| OL-04 | **Verify all claims with competitor evidence** — monopoly claims fail when tested | Analysis discipline |

---

### From PASHA Real Estate (Report #2 — First international, first Real Estate sector)

| # | Lesson | Impact |
|---|---|---|
| OL-05 | **Private companies have fundamentally limited data** — no PSX filings, no mandatory disclosure. Confidence grades must reflect this. More C-grade findings are acceptable if disclosed | Confidence architecture |
| OL-06 | **International engagements need macro context sections** — currency stability (AZN peg), oil dependency, government reconstruction programs all affect the company. Sector Snapshot section is more critical outside Pakistan | Report structure |
| OL-07 | **Brand dependency on partners is a signal, not just an observation** — "Borrowed Brand Equity" (OS-001) is reusable across franchise operations, licensed brands, branded residences, white-label manufacturers | New signal discovered |
| OL-08 | **Project timeline comparisons create instant credibility** — "Crescent Bay: 13 years vs. Burj Khalifa: 5 years" communicates more than any analysis paragraph. Use global benchmarks for context | Presentation technique |
| OL-09 | **Governance signals from investigative journalism are A-grade** — Guardian, RFE/RL, Carnegie, The Sentry provide verified ownership data that no company website will disclose. Always search investigative sources | Data collection |
| OL-10 | **Sector signal libraries expand with each new engagement** — PASHA built the entire Real Estate/Construction library from scratch. This is the system compounding. Each new sector costs effort once, then benefits every future client | System architecture |
| OL-11 | **Mall occupancy and hotel ratings are powerful "strength" signals** — 99% occupancy and 9.5+ Booking.com scores are irrefutable positives. Balance the "gut-punches" with verifiable strengths | Balance |
| OL-12 | **Counter-cyclical launch timing** (OS-004) applies beyond real estate — any company launching products that require buyer financing during a credit tightening cycle. Check mortgage/lending data for context | New signal discovered |

### From PASHA Real Estate V2 Revision (Client Feedback)

| # | Lesson | Impact |
|---|---|---|
| OL-13 | **Simplify ALL language for non-native English speakers** — CEOs in Azerbaijan, Turkey, Central Asia may not understand "Working Capital Physics" or "Revenue Concentration Risk." Use plain language: "Project Speed & Money" and "Income Types" | Report accessibility — CRITICAL |
| OL-14 | **ALWAYS define ORI and BDI at the top of the report** — First-time readers don't know what "ORI: 42" means. Add a visual scale (0–25 = Healthy, 26–50 = Some Risks, etc.) with a "← PASHA is here" marker | Report onboarding |
| OL-15 | **ESG and national contribution are STRENGTH signals** — PASHA's Victory Park, Karabakh hotels, reforestation, and COP29 involvement show alignment with national priorities. Must include these as positive findings, not just risks | Signal balance |
| OL-16 | **IPO plans are important governance signals** — A subsidiary going public (PASHA Bank 2026) changes the governance story from "private and opaque" to "moving toward transparency." Surface this prominently | Governance nuance |

## THE NON-NEGOTIABLES (EVERY ORBITAL REPORT)

1. ✅ All 10 signals analyzed and scored (even if data-limited)
2. ✅ ORI score calculated with transparent formula (show the math)
3. ✅ BDI estimate with justification
4. ✅ Blind Spot Register ("What You Believe" vs. "What Data Shows")
5. ✅ Competitive Position Matrix with 2–3 peers
6. ✅ Opportunity Map (Top 5 actionable items)
7. ✅ Prediction Registry with 5+ falsifiable predictions
8. ✅ Source citations for every claim with confidence grades (A/B/C)
9. ✅ Sector Snapshot with real numbers
10. ✅ Methodology section (sources, process, limitations)
