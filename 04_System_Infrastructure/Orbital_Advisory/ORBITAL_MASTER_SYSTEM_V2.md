## 1. WHAT ORBITAL IS

### The Core Question
> **"What does the market see about your company that you don't — and what should you do about it?"**

That's bigger than capital allocation. It covers:
- Competitive blind spots the CEO doesn't know exist
- Brand perception gaps between reality and belief
- Operational inefficiencies hidden by internal reporting
- Governance risks invisible to the board
- Geographic misallocation of resources
- Workforce signals indicating strategic drift
- Supply chain vulnerabilities not on any dashboard
- AND capital direction — as one output among many

### What Orbital Is NOT
- ❌ A dashboard (dashboards come later, after trust is built)
- ❌ A simulation engine (simulations support findings, they don't lead)
- ❌ Consulting (we don't give opinions — we give signal-backed intelligence)
- ❌ ERP replacement or BI tooling
- ❌ A one-time audit (it's recurring intelligence infrastructure)

### The Differentiation
| | **Traditional Consulting** | **BI/Dashboards** | **Orbital** |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Input** | Interviews + management data | Internal data only | External signals + internal data |
| **Bias** | Management narrative | Historical reporting | Outside-in reality check |
| **Speed** | 8-12 weeks | Real-time (of what's measured) | 2-4 weeks (Phase 0 = 2 weeks) |
| **Self-correction** | None | None | Prediction Registry + version tracking |
| **Blind spot detection** | Impossible (they use your data) | Impossible (only shows what's measured) | **Core capability** — finds what you're not measuring |

## 3. SCORING SYSTEMS

### ORI — Orbital Risk Index (0-100)
Measures overall structural risk to the enterprise. **Higher = more risk.**

```
ORI = Σ (Signal_Risk × Weight × Confidence_Multiplier) / Σ (Weight × Confidence_Multiplier)

Where:
  Signal_Risk = 0-100 per signal (0 = no structural risk, 100 = critical)
  Weight = 1.0-1.5 (varies by signal importance for the specific company)
  Confidence_Multiplier:
    Phase 1 data = 1.0 (highest confidence — verified by internal data)
    Phase 0 strong signal = 0.8 (multiple external sources confirm)
    Phase 0 moderate signal = 0.5 (single external source)
    Phase 0 inference = 0.3 (implied but not directly observed)
```

| Range | Label | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 0-25 | LOW RISK | Enterprise structure is sound |
| 26-45 | MODERATE | Some structural gaps — manageable with attention |
| 46-65 | ELEVATED | Material blind spots and vulnerabilities detected |
| 66-100 | HIGH | Serious structural exposure — urgent action needed |

### BDI — Blind Spot Density Index (0-100)
Measures how much the CEO doesn't know about their own company. **Higher = more blind spots.**

```
BDI = (Number of "Unknown" or "Surprised" findings / Total testable signals) × 100

Adjusted for:
  - Severity weight (critical blind spots count 2x)
  - Recency decay (long-standing blind spots are worse than new ones)
```

| Range | Label | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 0-20 | LOW | CEO has strong visibility — rare |
| 21-40 | MODERATE | Some gaps — typical for well-run companies |
| 41-60 | ELEVATED | Material blind spots — governance risk |
| 61-100 | HIGH | CEO is operating substantially blind — structural danger |

### How BDI Is Calculated
After Phase 0 delivery, we present findings to the CEO. Each finding is classified:

| CEO Response | Classification | BDI Weight |
|---|---|---|
| "We knew that, and here's our plan" | **Known & Addressed** | 0 |
| "We knew that but haven't acted" | **Known & Neglected** | +5 |
| "We sort of knew, but not to this extent" | **Partially Blind** | +8 |
| "We had no idea" | **Fully Blind** | +12 |

This makes BDI interactive — it's measured by the CEO's own reaction, making it personally compelling.

1.  **The Local Labor Arbitrage**
    - A senior data team in Pakistan costs ~PKR 10M/year. Orbital costs **PKR 6.5M once** + PKR 450k/month (total PKR ~12M in Year 1).
    - **Pitch**: "For the cost of one senior team, you get a 24/7 Executive Clone that has the cross-sector intelligence of the entire Magnus network."

2.  **The CapEx Pivot**
    - Frame the PKR 6.5M fee not as "work" but as an **Asset Installation**. In Pakistan, owners bargain on fees but pay list-price for assets (like machinery).

3.  **The ROI Lock**
    - We only pitch Phase 3 after Phase 1 and 2 have identified at least **10x the value** in operational leakage or trapped capital. This makes the 6.5M fee an objective "no-brainer."
