## 1. THE THREE WEAPONS

Magnus reduces information asymmetry through three things no one else combines:

### Weapon 1: Intelligence Collection
Public data, synthesized and cross-referenced across 6+ source categories, producing a structural picture of the company that no single source provides.

### Weapon 2: Contradiction Detection (NDI)
Systematic identification of gaps between what the company SAYS and what external signals SHOW. This is the crown jewel.

### Weapon 3: The Question Engine
For every divergence Magnus detects, it generates **specific interrogation questions** designed to test the company's narrative in a management meeting. The intelligence tells the PE firm what to worry about. The questions tell them exactly how to probe it.

**Example:**
```
MAGNUS FINDING: 
  "COGS guidance implies commodity stability, but LME cotton 
   prices rose 30% in the observation window."
  Confidence: HIGH (multi-source triangulation)

GENERATED INTERROGATION QUESTION:
  "Can you provide monthly raw material cost data for the 
   last 12 months, broken down by cotton grade and supplier?"

WHY THIS QUESTION WORKS:
  If management provides granular data confidently → signal was wrong 
  (document and adjust). If they dodge, generalize, or provide 
  inconsistent numbers → the divergence is real and the margin 
  compression thesis holds.

WHAT TO LISTEN FOR:
  - Hedging language ("roughly," "approximately," "in that range")
  - Redirection ("we focus on blended costs, not individual inputs")
  - Time delay ("we can get that to you later")
  Any of these increase the divergence probability by 20-30%.
```

**This is what no competitor provides.** Not the intelligence alone — the intelligence PLUS the surgical questions to verify it.

## 2. THE FIVE LAYERS OF INFORMATION ASYMMETRY

Magnus attacks asymmetry at every layer:

| Layer | What It Is | How Magnus Attacks It | Difficulty |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1. Unsynthesized Public** | Data exists but nobody combined it | Perplexity + cross-referencing | Easy |
| **2. Observable but Unmonitored** | Signals are visible but nobody watches | Customs, job boards, reviews, energy filings | Medium |
| **3. Derivable but Not Derived** | Intelligence exists only when you combine sources | Cross-signal triangulation engine | Hard |
| **4. Hidden but Inferrable** | Company hides it, but physics betray them | NDI + contradiction detection | Very Hard |
| **5. Unknowable from Outside** | Genuinely private information | **Magnus says: "We don't know this. Here's what to ask."** | Honest |

**Layer 5 honesty is a feature.** A report that admits its blind spots is more trustworthy than one that pretends omniscience.

## 3. CONFIDENCE ARCHITECTURE

Every Magnus finding carries a confidence grade:

| Grade | Meaning | Criteria |
|---|---|---|
| **A** | High confidence | 3+ independent sources, backtested signal, multi-signal triangulation |
| **B** | Moderate confidence | 2 sources, signal has some validation, logical inference |
| **C** | Low confidence | Single source, untested signal, speculative inference |
| **U** | Unverifiable | Insufficient external data; flagged as "ask in meeting" |

**Rules:**
- Every finding in a Magnus report carries its grade visibly
- MSRI score includes confidence-weighted signals (A-grade signals weighted higher)
- The Question Engine specifically generates questions for B, C, and U findings — because those are the ones that need verification
- "What Would Change My Mind" is mandatory for every A and B finding

### PHASE 2: CROSS-REFERENCING & CONTRADICTION DETECTION (Week 2-3)
*This is where Magnus becomes intelligent, not just informed.*

**What we build:**
- A module that reads the Perplexity dossier
- Identifies claims and facts across all 10 query responses
- Cross-references them for **contradictions and inconsistencies**
- Flags divergences with confidence levels

**How contradiction detection works:**

```python
# Pseudocode
for claim in management_claims:
    evidence = find_supporting_signals(claim)
    counter_evidence = find_contradicting_signals(claim)
    
    if counter_evidence and counter_evidence.strength > threshold:
        flag_divergence(
            claim=claim,
            evidence_for=evidence,
            evidence_against=counter_evidence,
            confidence=calculate_confidence(evidence, counter_evidence),
            grade=assign_grade(source_count, backtest_status)
        )
```

**Example output:**

### PHASE 3: NDI ENGINE + QUESTION GENERATOR (Week 3-5)
*The two most commercially potent components.*

**NDI Engine:**
- Input: Company's public narrative (annual report, press releases, management commentary)
- Process: Extract specific claims → match each against signal evidence → score divergence
- Output: Per-claim verdict (ALIGNED / UNVERIFIED / DIVERGENT) with trace chain

**Question Engine:**
- Input: Every DIVERGENT and UNVERIFIED finding from NDI
- Process: For each divergence, generate:
  1. The specific question to ask management
  2. Why this question matters (what it tests)
  3. What to listen for in the response (hedging language, redirection, inconsistency)
  4. What a "good" answer looks like vs. a "bad" answer
- Output: A "Management Meeting Interrogation Brief" — a ranked list of 10-15 questions ordered by importance

**This is the product's sharpest edge.** The PE partner walks into the management meeting with Magnus's questions. Every question is designed to either confirm or destroy a piece of the company's narrative. Within 30 minutes, the PE partner knows what's real and what's smoke.

**Deliverable:** Run `magnus interrogate "Masood Textile Mills"` and get the intelligence report + question brief.

### PHASE 5: MSRI SCORING + LEMON DETECTOR (Week 5-7)
*The mathematical backbone.*

**MSRI Calculator:**
- Aggregates all signal scores with covariance-adjusted weights
- Produces a composite score (0-100) with confidence interval
- Handles missing signals gracefully (adjusts weights, notes gaps)

**Lemon Detector:**
- Threshold logic: If NDI > 60 AND MSRI < 50 AND multiple high-grade divergences → LEMON
- Override governance: You always make the final call
- Sensitivity output: "If we remove signal X, the verdict changes to Y"

**Deliverable:** MSRI and Lemon classification running end-to-end.

### PHASE 7: BACKTESTING & CALIBRATION (Ongoing from Week 3)
*Run in parallel with everything else.*

**Method 1: Hindsight Reconstruction**
- Pick 3-5 companies with known outcomes
- Feed historical data through the pipeline
- Document: would Magnus have caught it?

**Method 2: Self-Backtest (Your Knowledge)**
- Run Magnus on companies you already know well
- Compare findings vs. your insider knowledge
- Right findings → validate signal
- Wrong findings → investigate → fix model
- New findings you didn't know → verify → that's the money

**Method 3: Living Prediction Registry**
- Every Magnus audit logs its forward claims
- Review monthly
- Build the evidence library over time

**Method 4: Counter-Narrative Stress Test**
- For every major finding, play devil's advocate
- Can you construct a plausible innocent explanation?
- If yes → downgrade confidence
- If no → findings are robust
